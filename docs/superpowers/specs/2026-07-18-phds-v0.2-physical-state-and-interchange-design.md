# PHDS v0.2 Physical State and Interchange Design

**Status:** Completed design record
**Date:** 2026-07-18
**Target:** PHDS schema v0.2.0

## Summary

PHDS v0.2 will add a canonical, dated representation of physical property state; replace single-address and unqualified rating fields with reusable, internationally neutral models; and add the dated financial and evidence records needed for lossless property-data interchange.

This is a clean schema revision. PHDS is pre-1.0, so removed or replaced fields will not receive deprecated aliases or backward-compatibility shims.

The design preserves the existing dependency direction:

```text
core -> entities -> profiles -> capture
```

Optional standards profiles live outside that chain. Core PHDS must not import or normatively depend on a national, regional, vendor, or asset-class-specific standard.

## Goals

- Represent what a property, site, structure, or space was like on a meaningful historical date.
- Keep stable real-world identities distinct from dated assertions about those identities.
- Make historical state sparse: an omitted field means unknown or unasserted, not inherited from the present.
- Distinguish canonical historical facts from the operational act of observing or extracting data.
- Support multiple property addresses, address roles, validity periods, and provenance.
- Represent condition, construction quality, and market classification without adopting one jurisdiction's scale as the PHDS scale.
- Add dated rent-roll observations, source artifacts, and verification attribution.
- Preserve semantic identity and information through all supported generated representations.

## Non-goals

- A generic event-sourcing or whole-profile versioning system.
- Consumer review queues, usability decisions, exclusions, or synchronization workflows.
- A normative address-normalization or hashing algorithm.
- A universal cross-property comparable-analysis document in core.
- A universal geographic-market taxonomy.
- Certification against any referenced external standard.

## 1. Architecture and boundaries

### 1.1 Stable identity, reusable facts, and dated assertions

The physical model will have three layers:

1. **Stable identity:** `Property`, `Site`, `Structure`, and `Space` identify durable real-world subjects.
2. **Reusable facts:** `PropertyFacts`, `SiteFacts`, `StructureFacts`, and `SpaceFacts` are LinkML mixins containing mutable descriptive fields.
3. **Dated assertions:** `PropertyState`, `SiteState`, `StructureState`, and `SpaceState` are independently identified assertions that reference stable subjects and reuse the corresponding fact mixins.

Fact slots are defined once and flattened into both the current-state entity and its dated state class. Identity, containment, and stable-reference slots remain on the stable entity rather than moving into fact mixins.

For example:

```text
Structure
  id: structure-123
  property: property-1
  ...StructureFacts flattened here...

StructureState
  id: structure-state-456
  subject: structure-123
  ...StructureFacts flattened here...
```

The stable entity and historical assertion must never share an identifier. This prevents JSON-LD/RDF node collisions and avoids conflicting generated-model identities.

### 1.2 Sparse snapshot semantics

State records are sparse assertions. If a historical `StructureState` contains `gross_floor_area` but omits `condition_ratings`, PHDS asserts the historical area and says nothing about historical condition.

Consumers must not fill omitted historical fields from current entities unless they do so as an explicit, separately attributed inference. Absence is not equality with current state.

### 1.3 PropertyStateSnapshot

Add `PropertyStateSnapshot` as the event-referencable dated aggregate:

- `id` — assertion identifier.
- `property` — reference to the stable property.
- `as_of_date` — required effective date of the asserted state.
- `basis` — open-vocabulary `CodeableConcept`; documented starter values include `at_sale`, `at_listing`, `at_lease`, `inspection`, `reported`, and `inferred`.
- `property_state` — optional `PropertyState`.
- `site_states` — zero or more `SiteState` records.
- `structure_states` — zero or more `StructureState` records.
- `space_states` — zero or more `SpaceState` records.
- `provenance` and `extras` through the normal entity conventions.

`SaleEvent`, `LeaseEvent`, `Listing`, and `Valuation` gain an optional `property_state` reference to a `PropertyStateSnapshot`. `PropertyProfile` gains a `property_state_snapshots` bundle.

Snapshots are physical-state aggregates, not generic history containers. Economics and transaction facts remain on their purpose-built entities.

### 1.4 Capture observations versus canonical state

PHDS will document two intentionally different time mechanisms:

| Mechanism | Meaning | Important time |
|---|---|---|
| Capture envelope | An operational act of retrieving, observing, or extracting data | `retrieved_at` or equivalent capture time |
| State snapshot | A canonical assertion about physical facts effective on a historical date | `as_of_date` |

A capture envelope may produce or carry assertions that become part of a state snapshot, but it is not itself the canonical property-history record. The dates may differ: a document retrieved today may assert facts effective years earlier.

## 2. Addresses

### 2.1 Address as a reusable value entity

`Address` continues to represent an address value that can be referenced by properties, parties, ownership records, and other associations. It does not by itself describe why or when an address applies to a subject.

### 2.2 AddressAssociation mixin

Add a reusable `AddressAssociation` mixin with:

- `address` — required reference to `Address`.
- `role` — open-vocabulary `CodeableConcept`; documented starter values include `situs`, `entrance`, `alias`, `address_range`, `former`, `mailing`, and `other`.
- `is_primary` — optional boolean within the association's subject and role context.
- `valid_from` and `valid_to` — optional validity bounds.
- `provenance` and `extras` through the containing entity conventions.

`valid_to` must not precede `valid_from`.

### 2.3 PartyAddress and PropertyAddress

`PartyAddress` will reuse `AddressAssociation` and add its `party` reference.

Add `PropertyAddress` as an independently identified association that reuses `AddressAssociation` and adds its `property` reference. `PropertyProfile` gains a `property_addresses` bundle.

Remove `Property.situs_address`. `PropertyAddress` becomes the sole canonical property-to-address mechanism. A primary situs address is expressed by `role = situs` and `is_primary = true`.

`OwnershipPeriod.mailing_address` remains an address applicable to the owner during that ownership period; it is not a property situs address.

### 2.4 Address hashes

Make `Address.address_hash` optional and add `address_hash_scheme` as an open-vocabulary, producer-namespaced identifier.

PHDS will not prescribe address normalization or hashing behavior. A hash without its scheme cannot be assumed comparable across producers.

## 3. Ratings and external standards

### 3.1 Generic Rating

Add a generic `Rating` type based on `CodeableConcept` with:

- required `system`;
- required `code`;
- optional `display`;
- optional `description`; and
- optional neutral `scope`, with starter values `overall`, `exterior`, `interior`, `component`, and `other`.

The rating records what system was used; PHDS core does not interpret one system's ordinal values as another system's values.

### 3.2 Distinct rating concepts

The following concepts remain separate:

- **Condition rating:** observed physical condition, maintenance, deterioration, repair, or renovation state.
- **Quality rating:** construction design, materials, workmanship, systems, and finishes.
- **Market classification:** competitive market positioning relative to a defined market and building type.

Convert `Structure.condition_ratings` and `Structure.quality_ratings` from strings to multivalued `Rating` collections. This permits overall, exterior, and interior assertions at the same time. Each collection permits at most one rating for a given `(system, scope)` pair. Their historical equivalents flow automatically through `StructureFacts` into `StructureState`.

Remove the shared `rating_system` field because each `Rating` carries its own system. Replace `CommercialDetails.building_class` with `market_classification: Rating` so that a market class cannot be mistaken for physical condition or construction quality.

### 3.3 International neutrality

Core PHDS will not define a universal condition, quality, or market-class enum. National and industry scales may be represented through their system URI and optional isolated standards profiles.

This permits use in any jurisdiction without treating a United States, Canadian, Japanese, Russian, or other local convention as globally normative.

### 3.4 Optional standards profiles

Optional schemas will live under `schema/standards/`, initially:

- `uad_3_6.yaml` for the UAD condition and quality scales.
- `boma_building_class.yaml` for BOMA office market classifications.

These schemas may constrain codes, labels, descriptions, and applicable rating concepts. Core, entities, profiles, and capture must not import them. Producers and consumers opt into them explicitly.

Each optional schema provides constrained `PropertyProfile`/container roots,
not only standalone rating classes. The UAD profile couples condition and
quality fields to distinct PHDS-defined system URNs, requires an explicit
`overall | exterior | interior` scope, and treats `interior` as the appraised
dwelling or unit represented by the containing `Structure` or
`StructureState`; it does not imply a core `Space` mapping.

The BOMA profile exposes separate metropolitan and international roots.
Metropolitan codes are `A | B | C`; international codes are `investment |
institutional | speculative`. Each basis has its own PHDS-defined system URN,
so a system and code from different bases cannot be combined.

BOMA classifications map only to `market_classification`, not `condition_ratings` or `quality_ratings`. Their market-relative and office-focused semantics must be retained.

If LinkML generators cannot express an optional profile consistently across supported targets, the implementation will keep the external mapping in validation metadata and documentation instead of compromising core types.

Near the bottom of the README, add a **Referenced standards and independence** section linking to:

- [RESO Data Dictionary](https://www.reso.org/data-dictionary/)
- [Fannie Mae property condition and quality guidance](https://selling-guide.fanniemae.com/sel/b4-1.3-06/property-condition-and-quality-construction-improvements)
- [Freddie Mac UAD and forms redesign](https://sf.freddiemac.com/faqs/uad-and-forms-redesign)
- [MISMO](https://www.mismo.org/)
- [Appraisal Institute Property Use Classification System](https://www.appraisalinstitute.org/insights-and-resources/resources/standards-of-professional-practice/professional-practice-samples-templates-and-documents/property-use-classification-system-pucs)
- [BOMA building class definitions](https://boma.org/boma-standards/building-class-definitions)
- [OSCRE Industry Data Model](https://www.oscre.org/Industry-Data-Model/Introducing-the-Data-Model)
- [ASTM E2018 property condition assessment guidance](https://www.astm.org/news/press-releases/e2018-revision)
- [RICS technical due diligence guidance](https://www.rics.org/profession-standards/rics-standards-and-guidance/sector-standards/real-estate-standards/technical-due-diligence-of-commercial-property)

## 4. Property interests and percentage conventions

### 4.1 Interests

Add `interest_conveyed: CodeableConcept` to `Transfer` and add `leased_fee` to `EstateType`.

Document the distinctions:

- `Property.estate_type` describes the estate generally associated with the property record.
- `Transfer.interest_conveyed` describes what a particular transfer conveyed.
- `Valuation.interest` describes the interest valued.

These values may differ legitimately and must not be automatically synchronized.

### 4.2 Percentages and fractions

Adopt and document these normative wire conventions:

- Fields ending in `_pct` use percentage points from 0 through 100.
- `cap_rate` uses percentage points; `5.75` means 5.75 percent.
- `Provenance.confidence` remains a fraction from 0 through 1.

Audit all decimal rate fields for ambiguous units and update descriptions and bounds. Introduce a shared `Percent` type only if it produces consistent contracts across all supported generators; otherwise use explicit descriptions and bounds.

## 5. Dated financial observations

### 5.1 RentRoll

Add `RentRoll`, following the dated-observation conventions already used by financial records:

- `id`
- `property`
- required `as_of_date`
- `unit_count`
- `occupied_unit_count`
- `occupancy_pct`
- `total_contract_rent`
- `total_market_rent`
- `rent_period`
- zero or more `lines`
- `provenance`
- `extras`

`PropertyProfile` gains a `rent_rolls` bundle.

### 5.2 RentRollLine

Add `RentRollLine` with:

- optional typed `space` reference
- optional typed `tenant: Party` reference
- optional `lease` reference
- `occupancy_status`
- `reported_area`
- `contract_rent`
- `market_rent`
- `extras`

Actor, space, and lease identity live only on their canonical records. A rent-roll
line must not copy a tenant name, space identifier, or lease dates beside those
references. When a source provides an unresolved identity, a producer may create
a provenance-backed canonical record or retain the original source artifact;
`extras` must not be used to recreate canonical shadow fields.

### 5.3 Rent-roll semantics

- Header totals are authoritative reported values; lines are not required to sum to them.
- All monetary values in one rent roll use one currency.
- A line does not automatically create a canonical `LeaseEvent`.
- For its effective date, a rent roll is the dated occupancy and rent observation. Current-state fields such as `Space.occupancy` or `CommercialDetails.occupancy_pct` remain current snapshots and do not override it.
- Date ordering, currency consistency, and other aggregate invariants not expressible in LinkML are enforced by the validation layer.

## 6. Provenance, artifacts, and narrative

### 6.1 Verification attribution

Retain the existing verification status on `Provenance`. Do not add a free-text
or untyped `verified_by` field: verifier identity is canonical party data, and
core cannot depend on the entities layer.

Add an inlined `VerificationAttribution` relationship in the entities layer
with a required typed `verifier: Party` reference, required `verified_at`, and
optional `note`. Add multivalued `verifications` to `Entity`. Verifier
references must be nonblank and trimmed, and resolve exactly once in
`PropertyProfile.parties`; validation traverses every Entity occurrence rather
than a fixed list of representative bundles.

Do not add `do_not_use`, exclusion disposition, reviewer queues, or other consumer workflow state.

### 6.2 SourceArtifact

Add `SourceArtifact` with:

- `id`
- `uri` or storage reference
- `media_type` using a MIME type
- `kind: CodeableConcept`
- `title`
- `original_filename`
- `content_hash`
- `hash_scheme`
- `page_count`
- `captured_on`
- `provenance`
- `extras`

`PropertyProfile` gains an `artifacts` bundle. `RecordedInstrument`, `Listing`, `Valuation`, and `Permit` gain optional artifact references. Capture envelopes may also reference artifacts associated with an observation.

Rights, licensing, and confidentiality policy are deferred until concrete interchange requirements justify a shared model.

### 6.3 Source-attributed remarks

Add optional `remarks` to `Listing`, `SaleEvent`, and `LeaseEvent`. Remarks may be source- or vendor-authored and are interpreted through the entity's provenance.

Do not add a general `Property.description` field.

## 7. Delivery sequence

### Phase 0: generator feasibility

Before changing the public model, prove across JSON Schema, Pydantic, TypeScript, and Rust that:

- fact mixins flatten correctly into stable entities and state records;
- state records retain independent IDs and stable subject references;
- `Rating` generates usable contracts;
- optional standards schemas can constrain core ratings without a core import; and
- serialization does not duplicate or overwrite stable physical identities.

### Phase 1: core physical and wire-contract revision

Implement as one coherent revision:

- fact mixins, state records, and `PropertyStateSnapshot`;
- event references and the profile snapshot bundle;
- `AddressAssociation`, `PartyAddress`, and `PropertyAddress`;
- removal of `Property.situs_address`;
- optional scheme-qualified address hashes;
- generic `Rating` and removal of the replaced string fields;
- property-interest additions;
- percentage documentation and bounds;
- capture-versus-state documentation; and
- the README standards links and independence disclaimer.

### Phase 2: dated observations and evidence

Add:

- `RentRoll` and `RentRollLine`;
- provenance verification attribution;
- `SourceArtifact` and artifact references; and
- source-attributed event and listing remarks.

### Phase 3: optional standards profiles

Add and separately generate/test the UAD 3.6 and BOMA schemas. Their outputs must remain optional and outside the core schema dependency chain.

## 8. Conformance and quality gates

Every new or changed structure requires:

- at least one valid example fixture;
- a focused counterexample that fails for the intended reason;
- JSON Schema and Pydantic agreement tests;
- TypeScript and Rust enum and round-trip coverage;
- portable regular-expression and schema linting;
- generated-artifact drift checks; and
- successful `just gen` followed by `just check`.

Snapshot-specific conformance tests must demonstrate that:

1. A current stable entity and its historical state coexist without duplicate-ID conflicts.
2. A state assertion points to the stable subject but has its own identity.
3. Omitted historical fields remain absent and are not filled from current facts.
4. Multiple snapshots for one property survive serialization without collapsing.
5. Event-to-snapshot references resolve within the profile.
6. Capture time and state effective date remain independent.
7. A canonical profile fixture survives parse, generated-model construction, and serialization without material information loss.
8. JSON-LD expansion preserves distinct stable subjects and historical assertions.

Rules that LinkML cannot express, including reference resolution, date ordering, rent-roll currency consistency, and primary-address constraints, belong in the project validation layer with corresponding positive and negative tests.

## 9. Clean-break changes

The v0.2 revision will remove or replace the following without aliases:

- remove `Property.situs_address`;
- remove the shared `rating_system`;
- remove `CommercialDetails.building_class`;
- replace rating strings with `Rating`;
- make `Address.address_hash` optional and add its scheme; and
- atomically regenerate fixtures and generated contracts for the new model.

## 10. Deferred work

Track, but do not implement in v0.2:

- A comparable-analysis extension in `schema/ext/comps.yaml`, with its own multi-property interchange document rather than `PropertyProfile`.
- Market-area and generic geographic-area entities.
- General assertion supersession and correction lineage.
- A machine-readable extension-definition manifest.
- Artifact rights, licensing, and confidentiality policy.
- Consumer review queues, usability dispositions, exclusion decisions, and synchronization mechanics.

For `extras`, document a lightweight producer-namespaced key convention. A registry or manifest should wait until demonstrated multi-producer exchange requires one.

## 11. Acceptance criteria

PHDS v0.2 is complete when a general property-data producer can represent and round-trip:

- stable physical identities and sparse event-time physical state;
- multiple temporal, role-qualified property addresses;
- system-qualified condition, quality, and market ratings;
- transferred and valued property interests;
- dated rent rolls;
- evidence artifacts and verification attribution; and
- source-attributed listing and event remarks.

The result must preserve meaning across supported generated formats without adopting application workflow, jurisdiction-specific normalization behavior, or a national rating system as core PHDS semantics.
